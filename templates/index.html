Interests & Hobbies

When it comes to keeping active, my absolute favorite pastime is bouldering at a local climbing wall here in Pittsburgh; After a few years of effort I can reliably climb at the V5-V6 range. I also enjoy hiking, belaying, and sprinting - almost anything to keep me away from treadmills and free weights, which I often find myself struggling to enjoy.

I'm obligated to out myself as an active and passionate Dungeon Master for the 5th edition of D&D. I've led multiple campaigns spanning nearly over half a decade, in addition to numerous one-off events and workshops. My ability to improvise, as well as organize and manage groups of people, echoes across my life in countless ways thanks to this fulfilling hobby.

While not nearly as active as I once was, I practiced and taught martial arts for over a decade before starting my undergrad, and continued on as an instructor for Pitt's Tae Kwon Do and Mixed Martial Arts student organizations. It remains an important part of my physical and philosophical makeup to this day, and my achievement of a 2nd degree black belt is one of my proudest.

Settling for a career in development would be nonsensical if I didn't love what I do. As such, I regularly tutor new and experienced programmers of all ages, lead community workshops, and spread the good word of open source development wherever it can be heard. I've made personal projects ranging from full-fledged VR games and graduate level educational simulations, to relatively successful automated algo-trading bots and tools for aiding myself and others in day to day tasks.
